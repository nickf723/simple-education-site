Expressions & Variables

Expressions are built from numbers, variables, and operations. Structure lets us rewrite expressions to reveal meaning or simplify evaluation.

Terms and Structure

Identify constants, coefficients, variables, and exponents. Like terms have the same variable part (e.g., $3x$ and $-7x$).

  • Like terms and combining
  • Order of operations (PEMDAS)
  • Evaluating with substitution

Simplifying Expressions

Use associative and commutative properties to regroup and reorder; apply distributive property to remove parentheses.

Example: $2x + 3 - x + 5 = (2x - x) + (3 + 5) = x + 8

Distributive Property

$a(b+c)=ab+ac$. Also factor out common factors: $6x+9 = 3(2x+3)$ to expose structure.

Equations & Inequalities

Equations assert equality between expressions. Solve by isolating the variable while preserving balance. Inequalities compare quantities and share many of the same steps.

Linear Equations

Use inverse operations; simplify first by combining like terms and distributing.

Example: Solve $3x-4=11$ → $3x=15$ → $x=5$

Be mindful of special cases: identities (infinitely many solutions) and contradictions (no solution).

Inequalities

Solve like equations with one rule: multiplying/dividing by a negative flips the sign.

Example: $-2x > 10$ → divide by $-2$ and flip: $x < -5$

Absolute Value

$|x|$ measures distance from 0. Solve $|x-a|=b$ as two cases: $x-a=b$ or $x-a=-b$.

Try it: When do you flip an inequality?
Whenever you multiply or divide both sides by a negative number.

Functions & Graphs

A function pairs each input with exactly one output. Representations include words, tables, equations, and graphs.

Notation & Evaluation

Use $f(x)$ for output at input $x$. Example: if $f(x)=x^2+2$, then $f(3)=11$.

Linear Models

Slope-intercept: $y=mx+b$. Rate of change $m=\dfrac{\Delta y}{\Delta x}$; intercept $b$ is the value at $x=0$.

Domains & the VLT

Domain is allowed inputs. Vertical Line Test: a graph is a function iff every vertical line intersects once at most.

Polynomials & Factoring

Polynomials are sums of terms $a_n x^n$. Operations preserve structure and factoring reveals zeros.

Operations

Add/subtract by combining like terms; multiply using distributive property and area models.

Common Patterns

GCF, trinomials $x^2+bx+c$, difference of squares $a^2-b^2=(a-b)(a+b)$, perfect squares.

Roots Connection

Factored form reveals zeros; if $f(x)=(x-r)g(x)$ then $x=r$ is a root and $f(r)=0$.

Systems of Equations

Find the point(s) where equations are simultaneously true. Solutions represent intersections when graphed.

Methods

Graphing (visual), substitution (replace a variable), elimination (add/subtract equations to cancel terms).

Solution Types

One solution (intersect), none (parallel), infinitely many (same line). Check by comparing slopes/intercepts.

Applications

Mixtures, motion, pricing—translate contexts into two equations in two variables.

Quadratic Relationships

Quadratics model curved growth/decay. Their graphs are parabolas with symmetry and a vertex.

Forms

Standard $ax^2+bx+c$, factored $a(x-r_1)(x-r_2)$, vertex $a(x-h)^2+k$. Each highlights different features.

Features

Vertex $(h,k)$, axis of symmetry $x=h$, intercepts from factored form, opening from sign of $a$.

Solving

Factor when possible; otherwise complete the square or use quadratic formula: $x=\dfrac{-b\pm\sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}$.
