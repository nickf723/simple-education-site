Core Distributions

Binomial

$X\sim \text{Bin}(n,p)$ counts successes in $n$ trials; $\mathbb{E}[X]=np$, $\operatorname{Var}(X)=np(1-p)$.

Normal

$X\sim \mathcal{N}(\mu,\sigma)$ models bell-shaped variability; $z=\tfrac{x-\mu}{\sigma}$ for standardization.

Poisson

$X\sim \text{Pois}(\lambda)$ counts rare events; mean and variance both $\lambda$.

Sampling & Inference

Sampling Distributions

Means/proportions vary across samples; standard errors shrink as $n$ grows.

Confidence Intervals

Interval estimates with a confidence level (e.g., 95%); not a probability about a fixed parameter.

Hypotheses & p-values

Set up null/alternative; p-value measures extremeness under the null.

Association & Regression

Correlation

Pearson’s $r$ measures linear association (scale-invariant, sensitive to outliers).

Least Squares

Fit a line by minimizing squared residuals; residual plots check linear model fit.
