The Foundation: What is an Equation?

An equation is a mathematical statement that says two things are equal. Think of it like a perfectly balanced scale. Everything on the left side of the equals sign ($=$) must have the exact same value as everything on the right side.

The Equals Sign: The equals sign was invented in 1557 by a Welsh mathematician named Robert Recorde, who got tired of writing "is equal to" over and over!

The Balancing Scale Analogy

Essential Vocabulary

  • Variable: A symbol (like $x$) that represents a number we don't know yet. It's a mystery we need to solve!
  • Constant: A number that stands on its own, whose value is known (like $5$ or $-12$).
  • Expression: A group of terms (like $2x + 5$) that represents a value. An equation is made of two expressions set equal to each other.

Check Your Understanding

Question 1: Is $7 + 3 > 9$ an equation?

Answer: No. It is an inequality because it uses a "greater than" symbol ($>$), not an equals sign ($=$).

Question 2: In the equation $4a - 7 = 13$, what is the variable?

Answer: The variable is $a$. It's the unknown value we need to find to make the equation true.
