Geometry: Shapes, Space, and Structure

Geometry studies properties of shapes and space. From angles and triangles to transformations and coordinates, it connects visual reasoning with algebraic descriptions.

  • Work fluently with points, lines, angles, and constructions.
  • Prove and use triangle congruence/similarity criteria.
  • Analyze polygons and circles, including length/area relationships.
  • Model with coordinates: distance, midpoint, slope, and equations.
  • Understand transformations, symmetry, and geometric invariants.

Points, Lines, & Angles

Foundations: undefined terms (point, line, plane), segments/rays, and angle relationships.

Angles

Complementary (sum $90^\circ$), supplementary (sum $180^\circ$), vertical angles (equal), linear pairs (supplementary).

Parallel Lines

With a transversal: corresponding and alternate interior angles are congruent; interior angles on same side are supplementary.

Constructions

Classical compass-and-straightedge: bisect segments/angles, copy segments/angles, perpendicular/parallel lines.

Triangles

Triangles are rigid—congruence criteria and similarity ratios power much of planar geometry.

Congruence

Use SSS, SAS, ASA, AAS; HL for right triangles. Base angles of isosceles triangles are congruent.

Similarity

AA/AAA establishes similarity; side ratios match, corresponding angles equal. Scale factors relate perimeters, areas, and volumes.

Right Triangles

Pythagorean Theorem: $a^2+b^2=c^2$. Special triangles (30-60-90, 45-45-90) have fixed side ratios.

Quadrilaterals & Polygons

Classify by side/angle properties; use interior/exterior angle sums and symmetry.

Angle Sums

Interior angle sum of an $n$-gon: $(n-2)\cdot 180^\circ$. Exterior angles sum to $360^\circ$.

Special Quads

Parallelogram, rectangle, rhombus, square, kite, trapezoid—know defining properties and implications.

Regular Polygons

Equal sides/angles; central angles and apothem relate to perimeter and area.

Circles

Central and inscribed angles, arcs, chords, tangents, and sector area create a rich geometry.

Angles & Arcs

Inscribed angle equals half its intercepted arc; central angle equals its arc measure.

Chords & Tangents

Perpendicular from center bisects a chord; tangent is perpendicular to radius at point of tangency.

Length & Area

Arc length $= r\theta$ (in radians); sector area $= \tfrac{1}{2} r^2 \theta$.

Coordinate Geometry

Connect algebra and geometry with coordinates to prove and compute.

Distance & Midpoint

$d=\sqrt{(x_2-x_1)^2+(y_2-y_1)^2}$, midpoint $\left(\tfrac{x_1+x_2}{2},\tfrac{y_1+y_2}{2}\right)$.

Slope & Lines

$m=\dfrac{\Delta y}{\Delta x}$. Point-slope $y-y_1=m(x-x_1)$; slope-intercept $y=mx+b$.

Transformations & Symmetry

Translations, rotations, reflections, and dilations preserve specific properties and reveal invariants.

Isometries

Rigid motions (translation, rotation, reflection) preserve length and angle measure; compose to map figures.

Dilations

Scale by factor $k$ from a center; preserve angle, multiply lengths by $k$, areas by $k^2$.

Symmetry

Line and rotational symmetry characterize figures; symmetry groups describe all symmetries of a figure.

Measurement & Similarity

Relate perimeter, area, and volume formulas; connect similarity to scaling laws.

Area & Perimeter

Rectangles $A=lw$; triangles $A=\tfrac12 bh$; polygons via decomposition or apothem.

Surface Area & Volume

Prisms/cylinders: $V=Bh$; pyramids/cones: $V=\tfrac13 Bh$; sphere: $V=\tfrac43\pi r^3$.

Similarity & Scale

Scale factor $k$ multiplies lengths by $k$, areas by $k^2$, volumes by $k^3$.
