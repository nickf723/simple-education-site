Linear Algebra: Vectors, Matrices, Transformations

Linear algebra studies vector spaces and linear maps. It provides the language for geometry in higher dimensions, data transformations, and many scientific computations.

  • Operate with vectors and interpret linear combinations and span.
  • Work with matrices: addition, multiplication, inverses, and rank.
  • Understand linear transformations, bases, and change of basis.
  • Compute determinants and interpret area/volume scaling.
  • Explore eigenvalues/eigenvectors and diagonalization basics.

Vectors & Linear Combinations

Vectors

Arrows with magnitude and direction; component form $\langle a,b\rangle$; addition and scalar multiplication.

Span & Basis

Linear combinations generate subspaces. A basis is a minimal spanning, linearly independent set.

Dot Product

$\mathbf{u}\cdot \mathbf{v} = \|u\|\|v\|\cos\theta$ relates angle and projection; also sum of componentwise products.

Matrices & Systems

Matrix Ops

Addition/multiplication, identity, transpose; solving $A\mathbf{x}=\mathbf{b}$ via elimination or inverse.

Rank & Solutions

Rank connects to solution spaces: unique, none, or infinitely many solutions.

Transformations

Linear maps preserve lines and origin; represented by matrices in a chosen basis.

Determinants & Eigen Basics

Determinant

Area/volume scale factor; zero determinant means non-invertible (collapses dimension).

Eigenvalues

Vectors scaled by a matrix: $A\mathbf{v}=\lambda\mathbf{v}$. Diagonalization simplifies repeated application.

Applications

Markov chains, PCA, differential equations, and more.
