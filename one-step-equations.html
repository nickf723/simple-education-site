Solving One-Step Equations

Our goal is to figure out the value of the unknown variable. To do this, we need to get the variable by itself on one side of the equals sign. We use inverse operations to undo what's being done to the variable.

The Golden Rule: Whatever you do to one side of the equation, you MUST do to the other side to keep it balanced!

1. Solving with Addition & Subtraction

Addition and subtraction are inverse operations. To undo subtraction, you add. To undo addition, you subtract.

Example: Solve for $x$ in $x + 5 = 12$.

The variable $x$ is having 5 added to it. To undo this, we subtract 5 from both sides:

$$ x + 5 - 5 = 12 - 5 $$ $$ x = 7 $$

2. Solving with Multiplication & Division

Multiplication and division are inverse operations. To undo division, you multiply. To undo multiplication, you divide.

Example: Solve for $y$ in $4y = 20$.

The variable $y$ is being multiplied by 4. To undo this, we divide both sides by 4:

$$ \frac{4y}{4} = \frac{20}{4} $$ $$ y = 5 $$

Inverse Operation Pairs

  • Addition ($+$) undoes Subtraction ($-$).
  • Multiplication ($\times$) undoes Division ($\div$).

Check Your Understanding

Question 1: Solve for $k$ in $k - 8 = 10$.

Answer: $k = 18$. You need to add 8 to both sides to isolate $k$.

Question 2: Solve for $m$ in $m/3 = 7$.

Answer: $m = 21$. You need to multiply both sides by 3 to isolate $m$.
