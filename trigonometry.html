Trigonometry: Angles, Ratios, and Waves

Trigonometry links geometry and algebra through angles and ratios. The unit circle unifies sine, cosine, and tangent; right-triangle definitions connect to real-world measurement; and trigonometric identities power algebraic manipulation.

  • Measure angles in degrees and radians; convert between them.
  • Use the unit circle to define $\sin,\cos,\tan$ and evaluate special angles.
  • Apply right-triangle trigonometry to solve for lengths and angles.
  • Graph sine/cosine/tangent and interpret amplitude, period, and phase.
  • Manipulate identities (Pythagorean, angle-sum) and use inverse trig.

Angle Measure & Radians

Degrees & Radians

$180^\circ = \pi$ radians. Conversions: $x^\circ = x\tfrac{\pi}{180}$, and $x\,\text{rad} = x\tfrac{180}{\pi}{}^\circ$.

Arc Length

For radius $r$ and central angle $\theta$ (radians): $s = r\theta$; sector area $= \tfrac12 r^2 \theta$.

Standard Position

Angles measured from the positive $x$-axis; positive counterclockwise, negative clockwise.

Unit Circle & Coordinates

On the unit circle, a point at angle $\theta$ has coordinates $(\cos\theta, \sin\theta)$. Tangent is the slope: $\tan\theta = \tfrac{\sin\theta}{\cos\theta}$ when defined.

Special Angles

$0, \tfrac{\pi}{6}, \tfrac{\pi}{4}, \tfrac{\pi}{3}, \tfrac{\pi}{2}, \dots$ with coordinates using $\tfrac{1}{2}, \tfrac{\sqrt{2}}{2}, \tfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$.

Symmetry

Even/odd: $\cos(-\theta)=\cos\theta$, $\sin(-\theta)=-\sin\theta$; periodicity $2\pi$.

Signs by Quadrant

I $(+,+)$, II $(-,+)$, III $(-,-)$, IV $(+,-)$ for $(\cos\theta, \sin\theta)$.

Right-Triangle Trigonometry

Definitions

For angle $\theta$: $\sin\theta=\tfrac{\text{opp}}{\text{hyp}}$, $\cos\theta=\tfrac{\text{adj}}{\text{hyp}}$, $\tan\theta=\tfrac{\text{opp}}{\text{adj}}$.

Solving Triangles

Given sides/angles, use trig ratios and inverse trig to find unknowns.

Applications

Heights & distances, bearings, incline problems.
