Calculus: Mathematics of Change

Calculus studies how things change and accumulate. Limits formalize approach, derivatives measure instantaneous rates of change, and integrals measure accumulation such as area. The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus unifies derivatives and integrals.

  • Build intuition for limits and continuity.
  • Differentiate common functions using rules of calculus.
  • Interpret derivatives as slopes, rates, and linear approximations.
  • Understand definite/indefinite integrals and accumulation.
  • Apply the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus to connect rates and areas.

Limits & Continuity

Limits capture the value a function approaches near a point, even if it is not defined there. Continuity means a function has no breaks or jumps.

Approach Idea

$\lim_{x\to a} f(x) = L$ means $f(x)$ gets arbitrarily close to $L$ as $x$ approaches $a$.

Limit Laws

Sum, product, quotient (when denominator nonzero), and power laws allow computation by parts.

Continuity

$f$ is continuous at $a$ if $\lim_{x\to a} f(x)=f(a)$. Discontinuities can be removable, jump, or infinite.

Derivatives

The derivative measures instantaneous rate of change and the slope of the tangent line.

Definition

$f'(a)=\lim_{h\to 0} \dfrac{f(a+h)-f(a)}{h}$, when the limit exists.

Rules

Linearity, product/quotient rules, and chain rule. Power rule: $\dfrac{d}{dx} x^n = nx^{n-1}$.

Common Derivatives

$\dfrac{d}{dx}\sin x=\cos x$, $\dfrac{d}{dx}\cos x=-\sin x$, $\dfrac{d}{dx}e^x=e^x$, $\dfrac{d}{dx}\ln x=\dfrac{1}{x}$.

Try it: derivative of $e^{2x}$?
Use chain rule: $\dfrac{d}{dx}e^{2x}=2e^{2x}$.

Applications of Derivatives

Derivatives reveal function behavior and solve real-world problems.

Critical Points

Where $f'(x)=0$ or undefined. Use first/second derivative tests to classify extrema.

Concavity

$f''(x)$ indicates concavity; inflection points where concavity changes.

Integrals

Integrals measure accumulation. Indefinite integrals form families of antiderivatives; definite integrals compute net accumulation.

Riemann Sums

Approximate $\int_a^b f(x)\,dx$ by summing rectangles; refine partitions to improve accuracy.

Indefinite Integrals

Find $F$ such that $F'=f$. Notation: $\int f(x)\,dx = F(x)+C$.

Definite Integrals

Area/accumulation with orientation: $\int_a^b f(x)\,dx$; units are “units of $f$ times units of $x$”.

Fundamental Theorem of Calculus

FTC links derivatives and integrals.

Part I (Accumulation)

If $F(x)=\int_a^x f(t)\,dt$, then $F'(x)=f(x)$ when $f$ is continuous.

Part II (Evaluation)

$\int_a^b f(x)\,dx = F(b)-F(a)$ for any antiderivative $F$ of $f$.

Example

Compute $\int_0^2 (3x^2)\,dx = x^3\big|_0^2 = 8$.

Techniques (Preview)

A taste of common tools; go deeper on a dedicated page.

$u$-Substitution

Reverse chain rule. Let $u=g(x)$, rewrite $\int f(g(x))g'(x)\,dx$ as $\int f(u)\,du$.

Integration by Parts

$\int u\,dv = uv - \int v\,du$; choose $u$ to simplify $du$.

Differential Equations

Separable forms: $\dfrac{dy}{dx}=k y$ has solution $y=Ce^{kx}$ (growth/decay).
